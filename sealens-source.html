Reef monitoring can’t keep up with reef loss

Coral reefs support over 25% of marine life, yet more than half are already gone, and up to 90% could disappear by 2050.

Reefs are disappearing fast

Driven by rising temperatures, pollution, and overfishing, reefs are declining at an alarming rate.

Reef health depends on fish populations

Changes in fish populations are one of the clearest signals of reef health and restoration progress.

Monitoring is slow and hard to scale

Manual counting from dives and videos is time-consuming, inconsistent, and difficult to scale across reefs.

When data is slow, action comes too late

Delaying critical conservation decisions.
