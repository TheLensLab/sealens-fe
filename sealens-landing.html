Reef monitoring can't keep up with reef loss

Coral reefs support over 25% of marine life, yet more than half are already gone, and up to 90% could disappear by 2050.

Reefs are disappearing fast

Driven by rising temperatures, pollution, and overfishing, reefs are declining at an alarming rate.

Reef health depends on fish populations

Changes in fish populations are one of the clearest signals of reef health and restoration progress.

Monitoring is slow and hard to scale

Manual counting from dives and videos is time-consuming, inconsistent, and difficult to scale across reefs.

When data is slow, action comes too late

Delaying critical conservation decisions.

FAQs

Everything you need to know

We've answered common questions about how SeaLens works and how your team can get involved

How accurate is the fish detection?
+
SeaLens uses state-of-the-art computer vision trained on underwater footage. Accuracy continuously improves as researchers validate and correct detections through the built-in review workflow.
What types of fish can SeaLens detect?
+
SeaLens currently supports 100+ reef fish species and is continuously expanding. Custom species can be added through dataset integration and model fine-tuning.
Can I use my own dataset?
+
Yes. SeaLens is designed to integrate with your existing data. You can upload your own labeled footage to improve detection accuracy for your specific reef environment.
Is SeaLens available for public use?
+
SeaLens is currently in a pilot phase with select marine research teams and conservation organizations. We are not yet publicly available but are accepting partnership applications.
How can my organization partner with SeaLens?
+
Reach out through our contact page or request a demo. We work closely with research teams, NGOs, and government bodies to find the right collaboration structure.
